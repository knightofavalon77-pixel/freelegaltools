Which State Should You Form Your LLC In?

Choosing the right state to form your LLC is one of the most important decisions a new business owner makes — and it's not always the state you live in. Delaware, Wyoming, and Nevada are popular choices for good reason: lower fees, stronger privacy protections, and business-friendly tax laws.

The Most Popular LLC Formation States Explained

Wyoming LLC

Wyoming is widely considered the best state for LLC formation for most small business owners. It has some of the lowest annual fees ($60), strong privacy protections, no state income tax, and excellent asset protection laws. Wyoming allows anonymous LLC ownership, meaning your name never appears in public records.

Delaware LLC

Delaware is the gold standard for startups seeking venture capital or planning to go public. Its Court of Chancery has centuries of corporate law precedent, making it the preferred choice for investors. However, Delaware charges a $300 annual franchise tax and requires a registered agent, making it less ideal for small solo businesses.

New Mexico LLC

New Mexico is the hidden gem of LLC formation. It has a low filing fee ($50), no annual report requirement, and strong privacy protections. It's one of the few states that allows anonymous LLC ownership without additional complexity — making it excellent for privacy-focused entrepreneurs on a budget.

Nevada LLC

Nevada offers strong privacy protections, no state corporate income tax, and excellent liability protection. However its annual fees ($350+) are significantly higher than Wyoming, making Wyoming a better choice for most small businesses seeking similar benefits at lower cost.

Should You Form in Your Home State?

For most small businesses that operate locally — restaurants, contractors, retail stores, local service businesses — forming in your home state is usually the simplest and most cost-effective choice. Forming out of state adds complexity including registered agent fees and potential foreign LLC registration requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to form my LLC in the state where I live?

No. You can form an LLC in any state regardless of where you live or operate. However if you operate primarily in your home state you will likely need to register as a foreign LLC there anyway, which adds cost and complexity. For most small businesses, forming in your home state is the simplest approach.

What is a registered agent and do I need one?

A registered agent is a person or company designated to receive official legal documents on behalf of your LLC. Every state requires one. If you form in a state where you don't live you must hire a registered agent service, typically costing $50–$150 per year.

How often do I need to update my LLC information?

Most states require an annual report and fee to keep your LLC in good standing. Wyoming's annual fee is just $60. Some states like New Mexico have no annual requirement at all. Always check your state's specific requirements to avoid your LLC being dissolved for non-compliance.
